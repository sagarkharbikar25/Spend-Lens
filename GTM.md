# GTM — SpendLens

## Target user (specific)

**Primary:** Engineering manager at a **Series A** startup (**5–15 engineers**) seeing their first **$200K–$500K annual AI tool bill**. They own team tooling budgets, feel margin pressure from the board, and do not have a FinOps function. Secondary: solo founder / CTO at seed stage with 3–8 people on Cursor + ChatGPT Team.

Not “all startups.” Not developers in general—**budget owners** who can act on a downgrade recommendation this quarter.

## What they search before finding us

- “ChatGPT Team vs Plus cost”
- “reduce AI subscription spend startup”
- “Cursor Business vs Pro pricing”
- “how much should we spend on AI tools”
- “Claude Team plan worth it small team”

Content and SEO should target **comparison + audit intent**, not generic “AI news.”

## First 100 users ($0 paid CAC) — 30-day plan

| Week | Action | Hours | Expected outcome |
|------|--------|-------|------------------|
| 1 | Post audit tool in 3 subreddits with **screenshot of real $ savings** | 3h | 40–80 audits |
| 1 | DM 10 EMs/CTOs in network with personalized 2-line note + link | 2h | 5–10 audits |
| 2 | Share in 4 founder Slack groups (ask mod permission) | 1h | 15–25 audits |
| 2 | Comment on 5 “AI cost” threads with helpful breakdown + link | 2h | 10–20 audits |
| 3 | Short LinkedIn post: “We audited our stack, saved $X” template | 1h | 10–15 audits |
| 4 | Guest comment on 2 indie hacker blog posts about tooling costs | 2h | 10 audits |

**Total time:** ~11 hours. **Goal:** 100 audits, 15 email captures, 2 high-value (> $500/mo savings) leads.

## Unfair distribution channel

**Credex’s existing credit buyers** are warm leads: every company that already purchased discounted AI infrastructure has proven willingness to pay and pain around forecasted spend. SpendLens becomes top-of-funnel before the next credit sale—email high-savings audits to that list (with consent) is a channel competitors cannot copy.
